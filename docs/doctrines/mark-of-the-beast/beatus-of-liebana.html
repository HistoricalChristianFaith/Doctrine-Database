Beatus of Liébana on the Mark of the Beast and the Number 666

Dates: c. 786 · Wikipedia
Summary: The Spanish compiler whose richly illustrated Apocalypse commentary shaped the medieval imagination gathers the whole patristic inheritance into a single set-piece: the Antichrist bears seven names, one per head of the beast, each summing to 666 — EVANTAS, DAMNATVS, ANTEMVS, GENSERICVS, ANTICHRISTVS, TEITAN, DICLVX — with an eighth, ACXYME, reserved for the mark itself.

Claims & evidence

The Antichrist has seven names, one per head of the beast, each = 666

"This is he who is named with seven names, for the seven heads that are the seven kingdoms subjected to him; and he will have an eighth name, which we have said above is ACXYME, the name in which will be made the mark on the hand and on the forehead. And by your charity, let us explain these seven names."

"One is EVANTAS — that is what is called “serpent” in Latin, for the one who deceived Eve first." "He has the second name DAMNATUS, for the one who brought in great damage to the world." "He has the third name ANTEMUS — that is, abstemious to strong drink — that is, wine — as if abstaining from wine." "He has the fourth name GENSERICUS, from the Gothic tongue." "He has the fifth name ANTICHRISTUS in all tongues." "He has the sixth name TEITAN, from Greek." "He has the seventh name DICLUX [DIC LUX = “say light”], from Latin, by which name we understand the Antichrist is expressed through an antiphrasis."

— Explanatio in Apocalypsin, on Revelation 13:17–18, in a working English translation, source. Beatus's seven candidates — EVANTAS, DAMNATVS, ANTEMVS, GENSERICVS, ANTICHRISTVS, TEITAN, DICLVX (plus the eighth, ACXYME, for the mark) — are each meant to yield 666 when their letters are read as Greek numerals; as he puts it, "the numbers are found in many, out of the Greek letters: “DCLXVI.” [666.]" The critical text is Roger Gryson, ed., Beati Liebanensis Tractatus de Apocalipsin, CCSL 107B–C (Turnhout: Brepols, 2012).

An eighth name, ACXYME, belongs to the mark on the hand and forehead

"he will have an eighth name, which we have said above is ACXYME, the name in which will be made the mark on the hand and on the forehead."

— same commentary, on Revelation 13:16–18, source. Beatus sets the eighth name apart from the seven "head" names and ties it specifically to the mark (χαραγμα) received on the right hand and forehead of Revelation 13:16.

Notes

  • Beatus is a compiler, not an inventor. His famous list simply gathers earlier candidates into one place: EVANTAS descends from Irenaeus's Ευανθας, TEITAN and DICLUX from Victorinus of Pettau, ANTEMOS from Primasius, and GENSERICUS (the Vandal king Genseric) from the interpolated Victorinus text. To these Beatus adds one of his own coinage — DAMNATUS, a Greek transcription (δαμνάτος) of the Latin damnatus, "condemned." Per Max Nelson, the eighth "name" is not a name at all: ACXYME is Beatus misreading the Roman numeral DCXVI (616) — the older variant reading of the number preserved in Tyconius — and turning it into the unintelligible Greek word ἀκσύμε ("=666"). See Max Nelson, "A Concealed Claudian: The Meaning of 666 in Revelation," Journal of Theological Studies 76.1 (2025): 109–137, at 109–137.
  • The abundance of names is itself the objection. That so many different names — Greek, Latin, Gothic, coined, and misread alike — can each be made to total 666 is, in retrospect, the clearest illustration of the "arbitrariness" charge against gematria: a number that fits this many candidates singles out none of them. The objection is weighed on the argument page for the leading identification, 666 = Nero Caesar; see also the doctrine summary, the mark of the beast and the number 666.
