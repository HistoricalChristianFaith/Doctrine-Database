Irenaeus on the Mark of the Beast and the Number 666

Dates: c. 180 · Wikipedia
Summary: Irenaeus gives the foundational patristic discussion of 666 — it stands in the oldest copies (he rejects the 616 variant as a scribal error), and he floats three candidate names by gematria (Euanthas, Lateinos, Teitan) while pointedly refusing to identify the still-future Antichrist. He names no emperor and does not read Nero.

Claims & evidence

666 stands in the oldest and best copies; 616 is a scribal error

"Such, then, being the state of the case, and this number being found in all the most approved and ancient copies [of the Apocalypse], and those men who saw John face to face bearing their testimony [to it]… I do not know how it is that some have erred following the ordinary mode of speech, and have vitiated the middle number in the name, deducting the amount of fifty from it, so that instead of six decads they will have it that there is but one. [I am inclined to think that this occurred through the fault of the copyists, as is wont to happen, since numbers also are expressed by letters; so that the Greek letter which expresses the number sixty was easily expanded into the letter Iota of the Greeks.]"

— Against Heresies 5.30.1, trans. ANF, source. Irenaeus grounds the true reading in the manuscript evidence and in living testimony from those who had known John, and diagnoses the rival number — 616, "one decad" fewer — as a copyist's slip (the numeral xi, sixty, mistaken for iota, ten). He is the earliest datable witness to the 616 variant and the first to argue against it.

Three candidate names, none asserted

"…for the name Evanthas (ΒΥΑΝΘΑΣ) contains the required number, but I make no allegation regarding it. Then also Lateinos (ΛΑΤΕΙΝΟΣ) has the number six hundred and sixty-six; and it is a very probable [solution], this being the name of the last kingdom [of the four seen by Daniel]. For the Latins are they who at present bear rule: I will not, however, make any boast over this [coincidence]. Teitan too, (ΤΕΙΤΑΝ, the first syllable being written with the two Greek vowels ε and ι, among all the names which are found among us, is rather worthy of credit… there is a strong degree of probability, that from among the many [names suggested], we infer, that perchance he who is to come shall be called 'Titan.' We will not, however, incur the risk of pronouncing positively as to the name of Antichrist; for if it were necessary that his name should be distinctly revealed in this present time, it would have been announced by him who beheld the apocalyptic vision."

— Against Heresies 5.30.3, trans. ANF, source. Three names satisfy the arithmetic; Irenaeus leans toward Teitan as "rather worthy of credit" yet expressly declines to "pronounce positively." His governing counsel is restraint: "It is therefore more certain, and less hazardous, to await the fulfilment of the prophecy, than to be making surmises, and casting about for any names that may present themselves, inasmuch as many names can be found possessing the number mentioned." The Antichrist is future and unnamed; no living or past emperor — Nero included — is in view.

The number recapitulates all apostasy

"Fittingly, therefore, shall his name possess the number six hundred and sixty-six, since he sums up in his own person all the commixture of wickedness which took place previous to the deluge, due to the apostasy of the angels. For Noah was six hundred years old when the deluge came upon the earth… For that image which was set up by Nebuchadnezzar had indeed a height of sixty cubits, while the breadth was six cubits… Thus, then, the six hundred years of Noah, in whose time the deluge occurred because of the apostasy, and the number of the cubits of the image for which these just men were sent into the fiery furnace, do indicate the number of the name of that man in whom is concentrated the whole apostasy of six thousand years."

— Against Heresies 5.29.2, trans. ANF, source. Before offering any candidate name, Irenaeus reads 666 typologically: the three sixes "recapitulate" the whole history of apostasy — the six hundred years of Noah at the Flood and the sixty-by-six cubits of Nebuchadnezzar's image before which the three young men refused to bow. The number is thus a summation of rebellion, not merely a cipher for a single name.

Notes

  • The header of the whole tradition. Irenaeus is the earliest reader we have of Revelation 13:18, and he treats the number as an unsolved prophecy about a still-future Antichrist — not a cipher for any past or present emperor. Every later patristic handling of 666 works downstream of this passage. His caution over the candidate names, and his choice to leave the Antichrist unnamed, set the tone for the tradition. See the argument on the 616 variant (he is its earliest witness) and on the "Nero Caesar" solution (the modern gematria he neither offers nor anticipates).
  • See also his page on the sibling doctrine. The same chapter that discusses 666 closes with Irenaeus's remark that the apocalyptic vision "was seen… almost in our day, towards the end of Domitian's reign" — the seed of the Domitianic dating of Revelation. See Irenaeus on the date of Revelation, which anchors on this same 5.30 passage.
