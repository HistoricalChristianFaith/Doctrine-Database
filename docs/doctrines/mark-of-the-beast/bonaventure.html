Bonaventure on the Mark of the Beast and the Number 666

Dates: c. 1274 · Wikipedia
Summary: A medieval outlier who abandons the name-hunt entirely. In the eighth of his Collations on the Seven Gifts of the Holy Spirit (delivered at Paris in 1268), the Franciscan minister-general reads 666 as "a cyclical number" signifying not a person but three intellectual heresies then unsettling the schools — the eternity of the world, fatal necessity (astral determinism), and the unity of the intellect. The beast's number has become a cipher for philosophical error.

Claims & evidence

666 as "a cyclical number" for three heresies of the philosophers

"Three errors are to be guarded against in the sciences, which destroy sacred Scripture and the Christian faith and all wisdom: one against the cause of being, another against the reason of understanding, and the third against the order of living. These errors are signified in the Apocalypse in the number of the name of the beast. It is said there that it had a name whose number is six hundred sixty-six, which is a cyclical number. The first group base themselves upon the circle of motion and time; the second, upon the motion of the stars; the third, upon one Intelligence, saying that it enters into and exits from the body. The first error concerns the eternity of the world; the second concerns fatal necessity; the third concerns the unity of the human intellect. All of this is heretical."

— Bonaventure, Collationes de Septem Donis, Collation 8. Bonaventure treats 666 not as a name to be counted out by gematria but as a "cyclical number" — a figure of circular, self-enclosed reasoning — and maps its three digits onto three errors of the Aristotelian philosophers: the world's eternity, astral fatalism, and the Averroist doctrine that there is a single shared intellect for all humanity. The number diagnoses a type of error, not an individual.

Notes

  • The polemical target: the Paris Averroists. The three "heresies" are precisely the theses Bonaventure and, in the same years, Thomas Aquinas were combating in the arts faculty at Paris — the eternity of the world, determinism drawn from the motion of the heavens, and the "unity of the intellect" associated with Siger of Brabant and the reading of Averroes. Bonaventure recruits Revelation 13:18 as a scriptural label for contemporary philosophical error; the collation was delivered in the spring of 1268, on the eve of the condemnations that would follow at Paris in 1270 and 1277.
  • Neither gematria nor an emperor. Bonaventure calculates no name and identifies no ruler. His reading is closer in spirit to the Augustinian tradition — the number as a moral or intellectual sign rather than a coded proper name — but he redirects it from false faith to false philosophy. He does not read Nero, or any Roman. On the range of decipherments, see the mark-of-the-beast timeline.
