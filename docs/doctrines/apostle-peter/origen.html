Origen on Simon Peter

Dates: c. 230 (quoted by Eusebius, c. 325) · Wikipedia
Summary: The first named theologian to record that Peter was crucified head-downward. His notice, from a lost commentary and preserved by Eusebius, also places Peter's mission among the Jews of the dispersion before his death at Rome.

Claims & evidence

Peter's mission field, and his crucifixion “head-downwards”

Peter appears to have preached in Pontus, Galatia, Bithynia, Cappadocia, and Asia to the Jews of the dispersion. And at last, having come to Rome, he was crucified head-downwards; for he had requested that he might suffer in this way.

— Origen, Commentary on Genesis book 3, quoted in Eusebius, Ecclesiastical History 3.1. The field Origen assigns Peter matches the recipients of 1 Peter (“Pontus, Galatia, Cappadocia, Asia, and Bithynia”), and the humility-motive — that Peter asked to be inverted — is the same one the apocryphal Acts had already supplied.

Notes

  • First, but perhaps not independent. Origen is the earliest datable writer to name the head-downward posture; but the motive he gives is identical to the romance Acts of Peter, and the detail is absent from the earlier Fathers who mention the crucifixion (Tertullian). Whether Origen preserves old tradition or repeats the legend is the crux weighed at Was Peter crucified head-downward?
  • The passage is one of the building-blocks of the “division of the world” scheme — Thomas to Parthia, Andrew to Scythia, John to Asia, Peter to Rome — that Eusebius took over; see the apostles hub.
