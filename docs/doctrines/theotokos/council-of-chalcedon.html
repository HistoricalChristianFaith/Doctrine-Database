The Council of Chalcedon on Theotokos

Dates: 451 (the fourth ecumenical council, convened by Marcian at Chalcedon) · Wikipedia
Summary: The council that wrote the title into the definition of the faith itself. Chalcedon’s two-natures Definition reaffirmed “Mother of God,” but placed it with new precision — Mary bore the one Christ according to his manhood — answering the very fear (that the title made the Godhead originate from Mary) that Nestorius had raised.

Claims & evidence

“Born… of the Virgin Mary, the Mother of God according to his manhood”

begotten of his Father before the worlds according to his Godhead; but in these last days for us men and for our salvation born [into the world] of the Virgin Mary, the Mother of God according to his manhood.

— The Definition of Faith of the Council of Chalcedon, source. The Definition keeps Theotokos but locates it exactly within the two natures it confesses. The one Christ is “begotten of his Father before the worlds according to his Godhead” and “born… of the Virgin Mary, the Mother of God according to his manhood.” The qualifying phrase — according to his manhood — concedes Nestorius’s legitimate worry (Mary is not the origin of the divine nature) while denying his conclusion: it is the one Lord, God and man, who was born of her, so she is truly the Mother of God.

Notes

  • Chalcedon is the timeline’s doctrinal seal: what Ephesus (431) made binding, the fourth council fixed within the church’s standing confession of Christ’s two natures. The title was never again in serious question in the imperial church.
  • The Percival (NPNF) translation renders the Greek Theotokos here as “the Mother of God”; the bracketed “[into the world]” is the translator’s own insertion.
