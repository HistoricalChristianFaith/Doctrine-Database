John of Damascus on Theotokos

Dates: c. 730 (An Exact Exposition of the Orthodox Faith III.12) · Wikipedia
Summary: The mature Byzantine synthesis. Three centuries after Ephesus, John gathers the whole tradition into a single dogmatic statement: “Mother of God” is not one title among many but the name that “embraces the whole mystery of the dispensation” — the surest guard, against Nestorius, of who Christ is.

Claims & evidence

The name that “embraces the whole mystery of the dispensation”

Hence it is with justice and truth that we call the holy Mary the Mother of God. For this name embraces the whole mystery of the dispensation. For if she who bore Him is the Mother of God, assuredly He Who was born of her is God and likewise also man.

— An Exact Exposition of the Orthodox Faith III.12, source. John states the logic of the title as a two-way inference: the name is true because the child is God, and confessing the name secures that the child is God — “the name in truth signifies the one subsistence and the two natures.” It is Cyril’s and Chalcedon’s point, now settled house doctrine of the Eastern church.

Why he refuses “Mother of Christ”

But we never say that the holy Virgin is the Mother of Christ… because it was in order to do away with the title Mother of God, and to bring dishonour on the Mother of God… that the impure and abominable Judaizing Nestorius… invented this name for an insult.

— An Exact Exposition of the Orthodox Faith III.12, source. The chapter is aimed squarely “against the Nestorians.” John will not accept Nestorius’s preferred Christotokos at all — not because the phrase is false in itself, but because (on John’s reading) it was floated precisely to unseat “Mother of God.” The polemical heat of “impure and abominable Judaizing Nestorius” is John’s, and marks how completely, by the eighth century, the title had become the boundary of orthodoxy rather than a live question.

Notes
