The Council of Laodicea on remarriage

Dates: c. 364 · Wikipedia
Summary: Laodicea softens the eastern penance and, in doing so, states the underlying rule as plainly as any canon: the twice-married have “freely and lawfully” married, and are restored to communion after only “a short space” of prayer and fasting.

Claims & evidence

Second marriages are “lawful” — communion restored after a short penance

It is right, according to the ecclesiastical Canon, that the Communion should by indulgence be given to those who have freely and lawfully joined in second marriages, not having previously made a secret marriage; after a short space, which is to be spent by them in prayer and fasting.

— Council of Laodicea, canon 1, NPNF2 vol. 14. The word “lawfully” is decisive: the second marriage is a licit union, only deprecated, and the “short space” of penance is a token, not a punishment for sin. The canon reads as the church’s settled compromise — the deprecation preserved, the prohibition refused.

Notes

  • Laodicea moderates the year-long penance of Basil and Neocaesarea to “a short space,” but keeps the same principle: remarriage is lawful and forgivable, never fornication. It is the canonical echo of the mean voiced by Hermas and later systematized by Augustine.
