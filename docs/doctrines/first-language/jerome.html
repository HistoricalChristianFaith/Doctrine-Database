Jerome on the first language

Dates: c. 391–393 (Hebrew Questions on Genesis) · Wikipedia
Summary: Jerome, the Church's great Hebraist, makes Hebrew the "mother of all languages" (omnium linguarum matrix) — the hinge of his lifelong appeal to the Hebraica veritas, the "Hebrew truth" of the Old Testament. In the Hebrew Questions on Genesis he reads the name of the Hebrews from Heber and the division of tongues at Babel out of Peleg's name, carrying the philological case for Hebrew primacy that Augustine would systematize.

Claims & evidence

The Hebrews descend from Heber; the tongues were divided at Babel

Arphaxad begot Shelah, and Shelah begot Eber. From Eber were born two sons: one was named Peleg, because in his days the earth was divided, and his brother's name was Joktan. Eber, from whom the Hebrews are descended, gave his son Peleg the name which means 'division', because in his days the languages were divided in Babylon.

— Hebrew Questions on Genesis (Quaestiones Hebraicae in Genesim), on Gen 10:24–25, historicalchristian.faith. Jerome reads Genesis's own etymologies as history of language: the people named from Heber, and the "division of the earth" in Peleg's day (Gen 10:25) glossed expressly as the division of languages at Babel.

Hebrew as the "mother of all languages"

Jerome's settled position is that Hebrew is the primeval tongue from which the rest descend. He states it in his Commentary on Zephaniah (on Zeph 3:18), where Hebrew is omnium linguarum matrix — "the mother of all languages" — and defends it in Epistle 18 to Pope Damasus §6, where he appeals to the whole ancient tradition that Hebrew was the one language of men "until the building of the tower" (postquam vero in fabricatione turris…), after which the tongues were divided. The conviction is of a piece with his life's work: the Old Testament's Hebraica veritas is authoritative because Hebrew is the original, not a derivative, speech.

— Commentary on Zephaniah 3:18 and Epistle 18 to Damasus §6; Latin and discussion in Michael Eskhult, "Hebrew as the First Language and Jerome," Revue des Études Augustiniennes et Patristiques 60 (2014), pp. 189–207, brepolsonline.net; the Epistle 18 passage in the Latin of Migne, PL 22, 365, reproduced here. (The full Latin sentence of the Zephaniah "matrix" phrase is not reproduced verbatim here; the phrase and its sense are what the scholarship attests.)

Notes

  • Jerome's Hebrew primacy is inseparable from his textual programme: because Hebrew is the mother tongue, the Hebrew Old Testament (the Hebraica veritas) outranks the Greek — the same conviction that drove the Vulgate. See his other appearances on the rabbinic-corruption and Old Testament canon timelines.
  • He stops short of Camp B (Syriac/Aramaic primacy): for Jerome, Aramaic ("Chaldee") is a later relative of Hebrew, not its parent — the reverse of the reading the medieval Syriac tradition would press.
