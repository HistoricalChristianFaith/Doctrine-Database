The Septuagint on How Tall Was Goliath?

Dates: c. 250 BC (the Old Greek translation of Samuel–Kings) · Wikipedia
Summary: The oldest translation of 1 Samuel 17:4 already gives Goliath's height as four cubits and a span — a champion of roughly 6 feet 9 inches, not the towering nine-and-a-half-foot giant of the Masoretic Hebrew's "six cubits and a span." In the Greek book-numbering the books of Samuel are 1–2 Kingdoms (older editions style them 1–2 Kings), so the verse falls in 1 Kingdoms 17.

Claims & evidence

Goliath's height is "four cubits and a span" (1 Kingdoms 17:4)

"And there went forth a mighty man out of the army of the Philistines, Goliath, by name, out of Geth, his height was four cubits and a span."

— The Septuagint, 1 Kings (1 Kingdoms) 17:4, trans. Sir Lancelot C. L. Brenton, source (also Brenton at eBible). Four cubits and a span comes to about 6 feet 9 inches — a tall warrior, but a man rather than a colossus.

Notes

  • The "four" reading is the mainline Greek tradition. It is carried by the great uncials Codex Vaticanus and Codex Alexandrinus and by the Lucianic recension, which together point to an Old Greek "four" from the start rather than a late Greek slip.
  • Outliers within the Greek and recensional tradition. Not every Greek witness agrees: Symmachus and Origen's fifth (Hebrew) column read "six," and the lone eighth-century Codex Venetus reads "five" — see J. Daniel Hays, "Reconsidering the Height of Goliath," Journal of the Evangelical Theological Society 48/4 (2005): 701–714, at 703–706, source.
  • Independent confirmation of "four." The Greek reading is not alone: it is corroborated by the oldest Hebrew witness, the Dead Sea Samuel scroll 4QSama, and by Josephus, both of which likewise give Goliath the shorter, four-cubit stature.
