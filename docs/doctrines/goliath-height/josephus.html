Josephus on How Tall Was Goliath?

Dates: c. 93 (the Antiquities of the Jews) · Wikipedia
Summary: An independent first-century witness to the shorter number. Retelling the duel of 1 Samuel 17, Josephus gives the champion's height as four cubits and a span (about 6 feet 9 inches) — a Jewish historian writing Greek from a Hebrew source, siding with the reading later carried by the Septuagint and the Dead Sea Samuel scroll against the Masoretic "six cubits and a span" (about 9 feet 9 inches).

Claims & evidence

Goliath is "four cubits and a span" in tallness

"a man of vast bulk, for he was of four cubits and a span in tallness, and had about him weapons suitable to the largeness of his body"

— Antiquities of the Jews 6.171, trans. William Whiston, source. The passage sits in Josephus's retelling of the challenge from "a man out of the camp of the Philistines, whose name was Goliath, of the city of Gath," who steps out between the two armies. His figure — four, not six, cubits and a span — agrees with the Old Greek and the Qumran scroll of Samuel.

Notes

  • A third, independent line of evidence. Josephus wrote at the end of the first century, before the oldest surviving Septuagint codices and independently of the Qumran manuscript. His "four cubits" therefore is not borrowed from either of them but stands as a separate witness — a third strand converging on the shorter figure alongside the Dead Sea Samuel scroll (4QSama) and the Septuagint.
  • His biblical text for Samuel was of the shorter type. Eugene Ulrich's study The Qumran Text of Samuel and Josephus (Harvard Semitic Monographs, 1978) demonstrated that the text of Samuel Josephus followed is close to the Hebrew tradition behind 4QSama and the Old Greek — not the later Masoretic type. His "four cubits and a span" is exactly what that textual family would yield.
  • The taller figure is late. The "six cubits and a span" of the received Hebrew appears only in the later Masoretic codices; the earliest reachable witnesses — Josephus, the Greek, and the Qumran scroll — know only the shorter Goliath.
