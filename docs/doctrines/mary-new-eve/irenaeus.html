Irenaeus on Mary as the New Eve

Dates: c. 180 (Against Heresies was written c. 175–185, in Irenaeus’ years as bishop of Lyons) · Wikipedia
Summary: Irenaeus takes Justin’s antithesis and makes it structural. Within his doctrine of recapitulation — Christ re-heading and re-tracing the whole human story to undo it at each point — Mary’s obedience is the deliberate counterweight to Eve’s disobedience: “the knot of Eve’s disobedience was loosed by the obedience of Mary,” and Mary becomes the advocata (patroness) of Eve.

Claims & evidence

The knot of Eve’s disobedience is loosed by the obedience of Mary

In accordance with this design, Mary the Virgin is found obedient, saying, “Behold the handmaid of the Lord; be it unto me according to thy word.” But Eve was disobedient; for she did not obey when as yet she was a virgin. … so also did Mary, having a man betrothed [to her], and being nevertheless a virgin, by yielding obedience, become the cause of salvation, both to herself and the whole human race. … And thus also it was that the knot of Eve’s disobedience was loosed by the obedience of Mary. For what the virgin Eve had bound fast through unbelief, this did the virgin Mary set free through faith.

— Against Heresies, III.22.4, source. Irenaeus is careful to match the two women term for term: each is a virgin, each betrothed, each acts by belief or unbelief — so that the reversal proceeds “by inversion of the process” that first bound humanity to death.

The Virgin Mary becomes the advocate (advocata) of the virgin Eve

And if the former did disobey God, yet the latter was persuaded to be obedient to God, in order that the Virgin Mary might become the patroness (advocata) of the virgin Eve. And thus, as the human race fell into bondage to death by means of a virgin, so is it rescued by a virgin; virginal disobedience having been balanced in the opposite scale by virginal obedience.

— Against Heresies, V.19.1, source. The image is of a scale righted: the same means — a virgin’s free response — that lost the race now rescues it. A caution on the word advocata: it is not Irenaeus’ own. The Greek of Book V is lost, so the book survives only in an early Latin version, and advocata is the Latin translator’s rendering of a Greek term we no longer possess — usually reconstructed as either paraklētos (“advocate,” but equally “comforter”) or, on the Ante-Nicene Fathers editor’s conjecture, antilēpsis, “a person called in to help or to take the other end of a burden,” making Mary the counterpart or balance of Eve rather than an intercessor in the later devotional sense. Because the original word is uncertain and the passage’s plain image is a balance of two obediences, advocata should not be pressed into a developed doctrine of Marian intercession without that caveat — a point weighed on its own argument page (assessment: weak as a proof of intercession; sound as the typological counterpart reading).

Notes

  • Irenaeus supplies the two elements Justin lacked: the recapitulation framework that makes the parallel a deliberate divine strategy rather than a pious coincidence, and the “knot loosed” imagery that later tradition would fasten on (the medieval “Mary, Undoer of Knots”).
  • The typology is still Christological at root — it is one panel of Irenaeus’ larger Adam/Christ recapitulation — but by naming Mary the advocata of Eve he gives her an active, personal place in the economy of salvation that the later Marian doctrines would reach back to.
  • On the biographical question of Mary’s continuing virginity, see the perpetual virginity of Mary; the New-Eve reading is a distinct, typological claim.
