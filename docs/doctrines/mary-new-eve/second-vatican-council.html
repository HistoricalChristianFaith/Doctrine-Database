The Second Vatican Council on Mary as the New Eve

Dates: 1964 (the Dogmatic Constitution on the Church, Lumen Gentium, promulgated 21 November 1964; its eighth chapter gathers the Marian teaching of the council) · Wikipedia
Summary: Nearly eighteen centuries after Justin, the New-Eve reading is taken up as the church’s own teaching. Lumen Gentium 56 quotes the fathers by name — Irenaeus above all — to say that “the knot of Eve’s disobedience was untied by Mary’s obedience,” and that the fathers “still more often… say: ‘death through Eve, life through Mary.’” The same section keeps the typology within its ancient bounds — Mary “freely cooperating… through faith and obedience,” not as a source of salvation beside Christ.

Claims & evidence

The council makes the patristic reading its own

Rightly therefore the holy Fathers see her as used by God not merely in a passive way, but as freely cooperating in the work of human salvation through faith and obedience. For, as St. Irenaeus says, she “being obedient, became the cause of salvation for herself and for the whole human race.” Hence not a few of the early Fathers gladly assert in their preaching, “The knot of Eve’s disobedience was untied by Mary’s obedience; what the virgin Eve bound through her unbelief, the Virgin Mary loosened by her faith.” Comparing Mary with Eve, they call her “the Mother of the living,” and still more often they say: “death through Eve, life through Mary.”

— Lumen Gentium, §56, source. The council’s footnotes cite exactly the witnesses on this timeline: Irenaeus (Against Heresies III.22.4) for both the “cause of salvation” and the “knot” sentences, Epiphanius (Panarion 78.18) and John of Damascus among the “death through Eve, life through Mary” fathers, alongside Jerome, Augustine, Cyril of Jerusalem, and Chrysostom. The reading is presented as patristic consensus, not novelty.

Notes

  • What the council affirms — and what it does not. Lumen Gentium frames Mary’s role as free cooperation “through faith and obedience” — the same active-but-subordinate place the fathers gave her. It stops short of the further proposal, urged by some in the twentieth century, that Mary be defined a “Co-redemptrix” sharing in the redemption itself. That extrapolation the magisterium has declined: Pope Francis called such a definition unnecessary (Guadalupe homily, 12 December 2019), and a doctrinal note of the Dicastery for the Doctrine of the Faith, Mater Populi Fidelis (4 November 2025, approved by Pope Leo XIV), ruled that “it is always inappropriate to use the title ‘Co-redemptrix’ to define Mary’s cooperation,” since it “risks obscuring Christ’s unique salvific mediation.”1 The New-Eve typology, then, is affirmed as the fathers held it — life comes through Mary’s obedience — while the stronger claim that she co-redeems is set aside.
  • This magisterial reception is the endpoint of a reading whose seedbed was laid by Justin, Irenaeus, and Tertullian; the council’s own citation of those and later fathers makes the continuity explicit.
