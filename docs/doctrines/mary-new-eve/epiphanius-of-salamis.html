Epiphanius of Salamis on Mary as the New Eve

Dates: c. 377 (Epiphanius, bishop of Constantia — ancient Salamis — in Cyprus, one of the most learned churchmen of his age; the New-Eve material is in his refutation of the Arabian Antidicomarianites, folded whole into Book III of the Panarion, composed c. 374–377) · Wikipedia
Summary: Defending Mary’s honour against a sect that made her an ordinary mother of later children, Epiphanius reads Genesis’ title “mother of the living” as pointing past Eve to Mary, and draws the antithesis in its sharpest fourth-century Greek form: Eve is “the occasion of human deaths,” Mary “the occasion of life”; the disobedience of the virgin in paradise is answered by “the obedience of grace… through the Virgin.”

Claims & evidence

Eve is intimated by Mary: the true “mother of the living”

It is Mary who is intimated by Eve, for she was symbolically given the title, ‘mother of the living.’ For Eve was called ‘mother of the living’ in that passage, and this after being told, ‘Earth thou art, and unto earth shalt thou return’ following her transgression. And yet, it was a wonder that she received the great title after this transgression. Physically speaking, every birth of human beings on earth is from that Eve; but here life itself has truly been born into the world of Mary, so that Mary brings forth the Living One and becomes the mother of the Living.

— Panarion 78.18,1–2, trans. Frank Williams, source. Epiphanius turns Eve’s post-fall title (Genesis 3:20) into a type: the name “mother of the living” is realized only in Mary, from whom Life himself is born.

Eve the occasion of death, Mary the occasion of life; the obedience of grace through the Virgin

But there is another marvel to ponder in connection with these women, Eve and Mary. Eve has become the occasion of human deaths, for ‘Death entered into the world’ through her. But Mary, through whom Life was born for us, is the occasion of life. And this is why the Son of God came into the world; and ‘Where sin hath abounded, grace did much more abound.’ And in the place from which death came, life got the start of it, so that there might be Life in place of death… And since Eve in Paradise fell into the sin of disobedience while still a virgin, the obedience of grace in its turn has come through the Virgin.

— Panarion 78.18,5–7, trans. Frank Williams, source. This is the classic Justin–Irenaeus structure — virgin disobedience reversed by virgin obedience — restated with Paul’s “where sin abounded” (Romans 5:20) woven in. Note that, like the earlier witnesses, Epiphanius makes the reversal turn on Mary’s obedience, not on any redemptive act of her own; Life is “born… through” her.

Notes

  • In the same book Epiphanius is the earliest extant writer to apply the Woman of Revelation 12 to Mary — and he does so with conspicuous caution, refusing to affirm whether she died, was martyred, or “remained alive,” since “Scripture… kept silence.” That qualified reading is weighed on the Assumption / Dormition, where Epiphanius stands as an early witness; here it matters only that his willingness to read the Genesis and Revelation “woman” texts Marially grows from the same New-Eve typology.
  • The immediate target is the Antidicomarianites, who denied Mary’s perpetual virginity; the New-Eve reading is deployed to show that so exalted a role forbids reducing her to an ordinary mother. Epiphanius appears on this wiki also on the perpetual virginity of Mary and the Assumption.
