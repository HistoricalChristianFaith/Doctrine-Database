Henry Preserved Smith on Who Killed Goliath?

Dates: 1899 (the International Critical Commentary on Samuel) · Wikipedia
Summary: The American critic's ICC volume states the majority modern verdict in one line: Elhanan slew Goliath, and the Chronicler's substitution of "the brother of Goliath" is a transparent harmonization. He even reads the difficulty backward onto 1 Samuel 17, noting that Elhanan's deed "would weigh somewhat against the present form of that narrative."

Claims & evidence

The Chronicler's "brother of Goliath" is an evident harmonization

"19. In another campaign Elhanan ben Jair the Bethlehemite slew Goliath the Gittite]; the harmonistic purpose of the Chronicler in making the victim the brother of Goliath is evident."

— Henry Preserved Smith, A Critical and Exegetical Commentary on the Books of Samuel (ICC; Edinburgh: T. & T. Clark, 1899), p. 377, source.

The intruded "oregim," and the restoration of the patronymic

"19. [jaʿrê ōrgîm] is hardly a man's name and the [ōrgîm] has plainly crept in from the line below. For [jaʿrê] it seems better to restore also [jāʿîr] with Chr."

— Smith, ICC Samuel, p. 378, source. On 1 Samuel 17 he adds that "the fact that Goliath was slain by Elhanan 2 S. 2119 would weigh somewhat against the present form of this narrative" (p. 152) — i.e. the Elhanan tradition is a datum the David-and-Goliath story has to reckon with, not the other way round.

Notes

  • Samuel-is-primary camp. Smith stands with Wellhausen and Driver: the Chronicler harmonized, so 2 Samuel 21:19 preserves the older tradition that Elhanan — not David — killed Goliath. He borrows Chronicles only for the plausible patronymic "Jair," not for its theology.
  • Pagination. The archive.org 1899 Toronto scan places the exegetical note on p. 377 and the textual note on p. 378; some later printings paginate differently, but the wording is identical.
