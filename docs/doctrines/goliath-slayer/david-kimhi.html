David Kimhi (Radak) on Who Killed Goliath?

Dates: c. 1200 · Wikipedia
Summary: The Provençal grammarian and commentator "Radak" reports both answers to the crux — the harmonization from 1 Chronicles ("Elhanan killed the kinsman of Goliath") and the Targum's identification of Elhanan with David — and then does something the harmonizers rarely do: he confesses that the identification puzzles him. Why Scripture would call David "Elhanan," he writes, "I do not know the reason."

Claims & evidence

He gives both readings, then admits the David-identification baffles him

…ולדעת יונתן אלחנן זהו דוד וכן תרגם הפסוק וקטל דוד בר ישי מחי פרוכת בית מקדשא דמבית לחם ית גלית… אבל מה שקרא דוד אלחנן לא ידעתי לו טעם:

— Radak on II Samuel 21:19, Hebrew via Sefaria (no published English translation is available there; the Hebrew is quoted verbatim). Rendered: "…and according to Jonathan [the Targum], this Elhanan is David, and so he translated the verse: 'and David son of Jesse, weaver of the curtains of the sanctuary, of Bethlehem, killed Goliath'… But as to why [Scripture] called David 'Elhanan,' I do not know the reason for it." Radak also relays the alternative that harmonizes via 1 Chronicles — that the man slain was Goliath's kinsman — setting the two solutions side by side.

Notes

  • Candor at the seam. Radak's "lo yadaʿti lo taʿam" ("I do not know the reason") is the medieval tradition's honest admission that the Elhanan-=-David reading, however authoritative, does not really explain the odd name — the same discomfort that drove the manuscript tradition to insert "the brother of" and drives the modern text critics to reverse the harmonization altogether.
  • Between the two camps. By reporting both the Chronicles-harmonization and the Targum-identification without forcing a choice, Radak stands between Rashi's flat "Elhanan is David" and the later verdict of Kennicott and McCarter that one of the two texts is simply corrupt.
