Julius Wellhausen on Who Killed Goliath?

Dates: 1871 (Der Text der Bücher Samuelis) · Wikipedia
Summary: The great German critic's monograph on the text of Samuel isolates the mechanical fault in 2 Samuel 21:19 — the intruded word "weavers" (oregim) — and, crucially, warns against the easy fix of simply importing the Chronicler's smoother reading. Where Kennicott treated Chronicles as the original, Wellhausen distrusts the Chronicler's tendency to iron out difficulties, seeding the view that Samuel is primary and 1 Chronicles 20:5 a later harmonization.

Claims & evidence

"Jaare" arose from the accidentally-intruded "oregim" of the line below

"19. Die Schreibung [jaʿrê] statt [jāʿîr] Chr. wurde wohl durch das folgende [ōregîm] veranlasst, dessen zufälliges Eindringen aus der darunter stehenden Zeile leicht zu erkennen ist."

— Der Text der Bücher Samuelis (Göttingen, 1871), p. 210, source. Rendered: "The spelling Jaare instead of Jair (Chronicles) was probably occasioned by the following oregim ('weavers'), whose accidental intrusion from the line below is easy to recognize." The corrupt patronymic and the intruded "weavers," on his account, are copyist's accidents in the text of Samuel.

Do not simply adopt the Chronicler's text for Samuel

"Bei der Tendenz der letzteren, die Archaismen zu vermeiden, wird man sich hüten müssen, einfach ihren Text auch für 2 Sam. zu adoptieren."

— Der Text der Bücher Samuelis, p. 210, source. Rendered: given the Chronicler's tendency to avoid archaisms, "one must beware of simply adopting his text also for 2 Samuel." This is the methodological hinge that separates Wellhausen from the Chronicles-is-original camp: the Chronicler is a reviser, not a purer witness, so his "Lahmi the brother of Goliath" is more plausibly a correction than the lost original.

Notes

  • Samuel-is-primary camp. Wellhausen supplies the critical rationale later spelled out by Driver and H. P. Smith and adopted by McCarter: it is far easier to explain Chronicles as a harmonizing correction of Samuel than to explain how a scribe with 1 Samuel 17 before him would have turned a clean text into one that has Elhanan killing Goliath.
  • The shared datum. Every camp accepts Wellhausen's point that the first "oregim" is a dittography from the "weaver's beam" at the verse's end. The dispute is only over the direction of the remaining corruption — and that is the question adjudicated on the argument pages.
  • Text note. The quotations are Wellhausen's own German; the English is an editorial rendering, not his words.
