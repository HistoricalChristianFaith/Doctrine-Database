Targum Jonathan on Who Killed Goliath?

Dates: c. 400 (Targum Jonathan to the Former Prophets; core traditions earlier, final redaction c. 4th–5th c.) · Wikipedia
Summary: The Aramaic rendering of the Prophets makes explicit the identification Josephus had only implied: Elhanan simply is David. Where the Hebrew of 2 Samuel 21:19 reads "Elhanan son of Jaare-oregim the Bethlehemite," the Targum reads the strange patronymic as a set of titles for David — "David son of Jesse, weaver of the curtains of the sanctuary" — so that the verse states, in as many words, that David killed Goliath.

Claims & evidence

Elhanan the Bethlehemite = "David son of Jesse, weaver of the curtains of the sanctuary"

"And there was again war with the Philistines at Gob. And David the son of Jesse, weaver of the curtains of the sanctuary, killed Goliath the Gittite, the wood of whose spear was like a weaver's heddle rod."

— Targum Jonathan on II Samuel 21:19, trans. E. van Staalduine-Sulman (The Targum of Samuel), via Sefaria. The Targum dissolves the problem by exegesis rather than emendation. The obscure name "Jaare-oregim" — literally containing the word oregim, "weavers" — is read as a description: David, of Bethlehem, who "weaves" (that is, provides for) the curtains of the Temple. On this reading Elhanan is a byname of David, and the verse no longer competes with 1 Samuel 17; it restates it.

Notes

  • The exegetical (not text-critical) solution. Unlike the later manuscripts and translations that changed 2 Samuel 21:19 (inserting "the brother of"), the Targum leaves the consonants and turns the difficulty into an epithet. This is the fountainhead of the rabbinic Elhanan-=-David reading carried on by Rashi and reported by Radak.
  • Wording note. The published Sefaria English reads "weaver of the curtains of the sanctuary"; older renderings give "weaver of the veils" and add "who was of Bethlehem," reflecting the Aramaic mi-Bet Lechem. The identification with David is constant across the renderings.
  • How the harmonizers differ. Josephus made the giant anonymous; the Targum makes Elhanan into David. Both keep a single Goliath slain by David — the reading modern text critics (McCarter, Driver) judge to be the later, harmonizing one.
  • Weighed as an argument. The identification the Targum founds — that Elhanan simply is David — is assessed on its own page, Is "Elhanan" in 2 Samuel 21:19 simply another name for David himself? (assessment: weak): the crux's earliest resolution and a genuine historical witness, but an equation no text makes, told against by a separate Bethlehemite Elhanan in David's Thirty (2 Samuel 23:24) and by the Gob setting of David's later wars.
