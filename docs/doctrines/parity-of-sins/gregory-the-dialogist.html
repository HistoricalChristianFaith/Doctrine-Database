Gregory the Dialogist on the parity of sins

Dates: c. 591 (the Moralia circulated by 591; Gregory kept revising, and the dedicatory letter is dated 596) · Wikipedia
Summary: Gregory takes the moral point of “guilty of all” without the exegetical apparatus, and gives it the image that carried it into medieval preaching: righteousness is a garment, and a man who is just in some things and unjust in others has not clothed himself but left one side bare. He is a witness to the practical force of the verse rather than to a theory of the gravity of sins.

Claims & evidence

Partial righteousness leaves part of the conduct naked to sin

Surely, when we are clothed with a garment, we are surrounded on every side, and so he is “clothed with righteousness as with a garment,” who defends himself on every side with good practice, and leaves no part of his conduct naked to sin; for he that is just in some deeds and unjust in others, it is as if he covered over this side, and exposed that one naked; nor are those henceforth good deeds, which are defiled by other evil deeds springing up.

— Morals on the Book of Job 19.32, source, expounding Job 29:14. The last clause is the strong one: good deeds are not merely outweighed by evil ones but “defiled” by them, which is as close as Gregory comes to the “guilty of all” logic.

Notes

  • Read carelessly, the clothing image could be made to yield the parity thesis — if one bare patch spoils the garment, why measure patches? Gregory does not draw that conclusion, and could not: his penitential theology is built on grading sins, and it is Gregory above all who transmits to the Latin Middle Ages the doctrine of a purgation after death proportioned to what remains unpurged. See purgatory, where he is a principal witness.
  • The image is doing the same work as Augustine’s answer — the whole is compromised by the part — without Augustine’s explanation of why. Where Augustine locates the unity of the law in the love that all the commandments hang on, Gregory simply asserts the unity of a righteous life.
