The Wisdom of Solomon on the Intermediate State

Dates: c. 50 BC · Wikipedia
Summary: Wisdom 3:1–4, a Greek-language Jewish wisdom book from Alexandria (roughly contemporary with Philo), gives the clearest Second Temple statement of a conscious, peaceful post-mortem rest for the righteous: "the souls of the righteous are in the hand of God, and there shall no torment touch them"; though "in the sight of the unwise they seemed to die," in truth "they are in peace," "their hope full of immortality." Their death is only apparent; they wait, conscious and at rest, for their reward.

Claims & evidence

The souls of the righteous are in God's hand, untouched by torment

But the souls of the righteous are in the hand of God, and there shall no torment touch them.

— Wisdom 3:1 (KJV/Apocrypha), Wikisource. The righteous dead are not extinguished but held "in the hand of God" — a positive, secure post-mortem condition, the blessed side of the intermediate state.

Their death is only apparent; they are in peace

In the sight of the unwise they seemed to die: and their departure is taken for misery, And their going from us to be utter destruction: but they are in peace.

— Wisdom 3:2–3 (KJV/Apocrypha), Wikisource. The text explicitly denies that death is "utter destruction" — that is only how it looks "in the sight of the unwise." The reality is that the righteous "are in peace," a conscious rest, against any reading of death as annihilation or unconsciousness.

Their hope is full of immortality

For though they be punished in the sight of men, yet is their hope full of immortality.

— Wisdom 3:4 (KJV/Apocrypha), Wikisource. The present peace of the righteous is oriented to a future: their "hope full of immortality." The intermediate rest is provisional, awaiting the fuller reward (Wisdom 3:5, "they shall be greatly rewarded").

Notes

  • This is the Second Temple background's clearest witness to conscious survival for the righteous — the data later pressed against soul-sleep (see the soul-sleep argument page). Wisdom's "they are in peace" stands beside the New Testament's "with Christ" / "in paradise" (see The New Testament).
  • The same book supplies the giants / cult-of-the-dead data on the Nephilim timeline; see The Wisdom of Solomon on the Giants and the Cult of the Dead. Like 2 Maccabees it is deuterocanonical (received in the Catholic and Orthodox canons, classed apocryphal by Jews and Protestants).
