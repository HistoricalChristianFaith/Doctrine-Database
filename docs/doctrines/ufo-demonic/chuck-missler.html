Chuck Missler and Mark Eastman on UFOs and the Demonic

Dates: c. 1997 (Missler 1934–2018) · Wikipedia
Summary: Chuck Missler — an engineer and Bible teacher, founder of the Koinonia House ministry — and the physician Mark Eastman are the writers who fused the interdimensional UFO thesis to the Genesis 6 Nephilim for a popular evangelical audience. In Alien Encounters: The Secret Behind the UFO Phenomenon (1997) they argue that the "real" UFOs are not extraterrestrial biology but demonic and dimension-crossing, that alien abduction re-enacts the Nephilim episode of Genesis 6, and that the whole phenomenon is an end-times "days of Noah" deception. Their book leans openly on Jacques Vallée's fairies-to-aliens continuity.

Claims & evidence

The "real" UFOs are demonic and dimension-crossing, not extraterrestrial

Our working premise in this series of articles has been that the "real" UFOs are demonic in nature, and that they enjoy some apparent ability to transit dimensions into and out of our own physical reality.

— Post-Flood Nephilim, khouse.org. This is the thesis of Alien Encounters stated in Missler's own words: the phenomenon is real, but its occupants are spiritual beings able to cross into and out of physical reality — exactly Vallée's "interdimensional" behavior, re-identified as the demonic.1

Alien abduction is a recurrence of the Genesis 6 Nephilim

The Nephilim were the strange hybrids of Genesis 6, apparently the principal reason for the judgment of the Flood of Noah's time.

— Post-Flood Nephilim, khouse.org. On Missler and Eastman's reading, the "sons of God" of Genesis 6 produced hybrid offspring, and the reproductive procedures widely reported in abduction accounts (extraction of ova and sperm, hybrid gestation) look like the same fallen-angel program resumed. The angelic/Enochic reading of Genesis 6 this rests on is the one traced in depth on the Nephilim timeline; whether the demons are the dead giants' spirits is weighed there on the unclean-spirits argument page. Whether the modern abduction reports really are that program resumed — the "days of Noah" recurrence — is weighed on this doctrine's abduction-hybridization argument page (assessment: weak).2

The phenomenon is an end-times "days of Noah" deception

But as the days of Noah were, so shall also the coming of the Son of man be. Matthew 24:37. This is one of the reasons why some conjecture that the UFO phenomena may be a contemporary manifestation of a recurrence of these same hybrids.

— Post-Flood Nephilim, khouse.org. Reading Jesus's "as the days of Noah" (Matthew 24:37) as a promise that the antediluvian conditions — including the Nephilim — would return before the end, Missler frames modern ufology as a precursor of that deception: "There are many who believe that the recent 'alien' involvements are also demonic and are just another precursor to the end-time."3

Notes

  • The book. Alien Encounters: The Secret Behind the UFO Phenomenon (Koinonia House, 1997) is organized in three parts, the third — "The Biblical View" — containing the chapter "The Return of the Nephilim," the title Koinonia House also gave the condensed briefing package on the same theme.4
  • Debt to Vallée. The book's own index runs "Jacques Vallée," "Magonia," "interdimensional," "demons," "fallen angels," and "Nephilim" together — it is the clearest early instance of a Christian writer taking Vallée's secular continuity (fairies → apparitions → aliens) and relabeling the "other dimension" as the spiritual realm.
  • "Stopped in the name of Jesus." Missler and Eastman also report the strand — developed by the CE4 Research Group's Joe Jordan and later popularized by Gary Bates — that abduction episodes reportedly halt when the experiencer calls on the name of Jesus Christ. That claim is a distinct sub-argument adjudicated in this doctrine's Arguments section, not a settled fact.
  • Missler later wrote the introduction to Tom Horn and Cris Putnam's Exo-Vaticana (2013), extending the same reading to the "alien disclosure" theme.
