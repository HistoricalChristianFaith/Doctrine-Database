Tom Horn on UFOs and the Demonic

Dates: c. 2013 (1957–2023) · SkyWatchTV
Summary: Thomas R. Horn — a Christian author and publisher, founder of Defender Publishing and SkyWatchTV — extended the demonic-UFO reading into a specific eschatological charge: that "extraterrestrial disclosure" is being prepared as an end-times deception, and that the Vatican has positioned itself to broker a coming "alien savior." His book Exo-Vaticana (2013, with Cris Putnam) is the fullest statement, tying the Vatican's astronomical work and its openness to extraterrestrial life to the fallen-angel deception.

Claims & evidence

"Disclosure" is being staged as an end-times deception — "salvation from above"

Rome has wittingly or unwittingly set itself up to become the agent of mass end-times deception regarding "salvation from above."

— Thomas Horn and Cris Putnam, Exo-Vaticana (2013), quoted in "The Vatican's Savior?", khouse.org. Horn and Putnam argue the Roman Church "has intentionally positioned itself to be the religious authority on, and ecclesiastical benefactor of, Official Disclosure" — so that when an "alien" contact narrative arrives, an existing religious authority is ready to baptize it.1

The coming "savior" is Daniel's "alien god"

[The authors] expose the elitists and intellectuals who are planning to assimilate mankind under a coming "savior," one whom the prophet Daniel foresaw as "an alien god."

— Exo-Vaticana, publisher's description, book listing. Horn reads Daniel 11:38–39 ("a god whom his fathers knew not… a strange god") as a prophecy of the alien-savior deception, making the "disclosure" of extraterrestrial contact the mask for the worship of a fallen power.2

The "L.U.C.I.F.E.R." telescope on Mount Graham

Horn and Putnam make heavy narrative use of an instrument named LUCIFER installed on Mount Graham, Arizona, treating the name as a tell that the Vatican's astronomy is bound up with the coming deception. The underlying facts, however, cut against the framing: LUCIFER (later renamed LUCI) is a German-built near-infrared camera-spectrograph on the Large Binocular Telescope — a US/German/Italian instrument, not owned or operated by the Vatican — whose acronym is a mundane one ("LBT Near-Infrared Spectroscopic Utility with Camera and Integral Field Unit for Extragalactic Research"). It merely shares the mountaintop with the separate Vatican Advanced Technology Telescope. The rhetorical force of Horn's "Vatican's LUCIFER" rests on conflating two distinct telescopes, a conflation weighed adversarially in this doctrine's Arguments section.3

Notes

  • The Vatican-astronomer quotes. Horn and Putnam build their case partly on interviews with and statements by Vatican astronomers (e.g. Br. Guy Consolmagno's "we're going to find out that everything is truer in ways we couldn't even yet have imagined"). Those same astronomers hold an ordinary Catholic exotheology — that any real extraterrestrial life would be part of God's creation — and have publicly rejected Horn and Putnam's "alien savior" spin; the counter-voices are set out on the doctrine timeline.
  • Continuity with Missler. Chuck Missler wrote the introduction to Exo-Vaticana, and Horn's "alien disclosure as end-times deception" reading is a direct development of the Missler–Eastman "days of Noah" framing. Horn's contribution is to fix the deception on a specific institutional target (the Vatican) and a specific eschatological schedule (the Petrus Romanus "final pope" thesis of his 2012 book).
  • The sensational "Vatican plan for an alien savior" thesis is Horn and Putnam's own; the factual core it rests on (that the Vatican Observatory studies the heavens and that Catholic theologians are open to ET life) is real, but the inference to a coordinated deception plan is the disputed step.
